1962

BIRTH

Nicolás Maduro Moros born in Caracas, Venezuela. Son of a union leader and a teacher.

1998

ENTERING POLITICS

Elected to the Venezuelan Chamber of Deputies as a member of the Fifth Republic Movement (MVR).

2005

NATIONAL ASSEMBLY SPEAKER

Became Speaker (President) of the National Assembly, consolidating his position in the Chávez government.

2006

FOREIGN MINISTER

Appointed Minister of Foreign Affairs by Hugo Chávez. Served for 6 years, building international alliances.

2012

VICE PRESIDENT

Appointed Vice President by Chávez after winning re-election. Became Chávez's designated successor.

2013

CHÁVEZ DIES

Hugo Chávez dies after a two-year battle with cancer. Maduro becomes acting president.

2013

WINS PRESIDENCY

Wins special election to complete Chávez's term with 50.6% of the vote. Narrow margin over Henrique Capriles.

2018

RE-ELECTED (DISPUTED)

Wins second term in election boycotted by opposition. US, EU, and OAS reject results as illegitimate.

2020

2020 INDICTMENT

Trump DOJ charges Maduro with narco-terrorism, cocaine importation, and leading "Cartel de los Soles." $15M bounty offered.

2024

2024 ELECTION FRAUD

Claims victory with 51.2% but opposition candidate Edmundo González likely won. Carter Center and US reject results.

2026

OPERATION ABSOLUTE RESOLVE

US military surprise attack begins 2 a.m. in Caracas. Bombs suppress air defenses. Maduro and wife Cilia Flores captured after 2h 28m war.

2026

FIRST HEARING

Maduro and Flores arraigned in Lower Manhattan federal courthouse. Both plead not guilty to all charges including narco-terrorism.

2026

DIPLOMATIC TIES RESTORED

After 7 years of broken relations, US and Venezuela reestablish diplomatic and consular ties. 659 political prisoners released.
